We were tasked with choosing and redesigning a non profits organizations website. As we were researching, the team lead had found through this friend the organization called UP3 what the do is:

Up3 helps disadvantaged teens in foster care discover their potential by cultivating the individual strengths and passions they already possess. Personalized programs and financial assistance will help them build critical life skills that will set them up for success upon aging out of the system.
During the discovery phase of the project, we conducted user interviews to gain a better understanding of how users function when they associate with an organization, and to understand how we might tackle the task at hand

Some of the research Questions Include:

What factors help you determine which nonprofit organizations to go with?Â 

Do you feel like a nonprofit organizationâ€™s online presence is important to you? If yes, why is this important you? If no, why isnâ€™t this important you?

What factors help you determine whether a nonprofit organization is trustworthy and credible?

We conducted about 5 interviews and we were curious to see how each person had their own way wanting to either work with or donate to an organizations,

Some of the key insights that we received from the interviews is that trust is very important to them, how the money was being used and where it was headed to.

What we changed was the way you interact with the information given before the redesign, how we consolidated so we can clearly define and focus on the information that matters.
Before we conducted our interviews we scoped out how people work with organizations

We did the survey because we wanted to gain on how people operated when they wanted to associated with an organization. We found that people needed to see a strong online presence, they wanted to how their money was being used and where it was going to, and how connected they had felt to the organization.

We also saw that many people donated/volunteered often, so we wanted to take that into consideration when we redesigned UP3's website.

What I have Learnt
Based on the interviews/workshop we set up three personas. We referred to them throughout the entire product development process.

Based on the interviews we conducted, we created Rima Robinson, and as we went through the defining we were referring back to them throughout the process.

I Started this part of the process, with low fidelity wireframes, so we could start structuring out website and get a visual for how the website could look like. The point of the sketches were to brainstorms and ideate the website.

Seeing as how the users wanted a better online presence we tried to create a more contemporary and usable website.

The sketches were the basis for how we moved forward with project at hand.
I started drawing up the version of the website that I saw in my head, once that was drawn I showed it to my teammates received their feedback, and we decided to move along the process.

These mid-fi wireframe worked for the website.

We used them for testing to start received feed back and to create the iterations needed, so that we could make the best decisions for the website.

We created these wireframes on Adobe XD, and then conducted one on one tests, as we had certain tasks we needed completed, that could not have been if we used a program that limited that.
